Who are we?

Team

Genesis of Robotics is a primarily home-school robotics team of 15 participating in the 2014 BEST Robotics competition, BladeRunner. We are a team of engineers, artists, and designers.

Structure

The design engineers on our team plan a robot to complete the competition's challenge, and the build engineers manufacture that design. The marketing team is responsible for designing and building the marketing exhibit, creating a T-shirt, and promoting the team's product. Most importantly, we are a unified team of friends with a common goal.

Values

Our team's greatest desire is to bring glory to God and represent Christ in everything we do. Every idea, every victory comes from God and could only be possible through him. After all, He is the Master Designer.

What is BEST?

BEST (Boosting Engineering Science and Technology) gives middle school and high school students nationwide the valuable opportunity to learn about real-world engineering through the design, composition, and marketing of a robot.

The mission of BEST Inc. is to "inspire students to pursue careers in engineering, science, technology, and mathematics through robotics design."
The significance of BEST lies not only in the great opportunity for the students to grow their leadership, teamwork, problem solving, and abstract thinking skills, but also as a way for students to explore various career paths as they prepare for their future in the real world.

Learn more at BESTinc.org

What's our challenge?

2014's BEST competition, BladeRunner, is based on the growing wind power industry. Teams are given six weeks to design and build a robot, create a technical notebook, and prepare a tradeshow exhibit and marketing presentation. Then, they compete in a timed environment to assemble wind turbines by remotely controlling a robot to manipulate and assemble the heavy and cumbersome parts.

The Field

The robot must transport wind turbine components to the assembly area over either a bridge or damaged road. The driver controls the robot from alongside the field, while the spotter manipulates the turbine towers. Before assembly, the robot must obtain an Over-Size/Over-Weight permit from the permit station. Optionally, a team may choose to cooperate with the team adjecent to them, allowing them to share parts and points.

Large Turbine

The robot installs three blades into the lowered large turbine tower. The spotter then raises the tower and positions the blades correctly.

Small Turbine

The robot installs the nacelle onto the lowered small turbine tower. The robot then installs the blade assembly onto the nacelle before the spotter raises the tower. The robot also removes the shipping band.

Chicken Habitats

The robot avoids knocking over the prairie chicken habitats in its way, and may move them to a safe area for extra points.

History

The Genesis of Robotics Team was organized in 2012 for the BEST Warp XX Challenge. The team formed because of a shared interest in robotics and competition. The BEST competition gave Genesis of Robotics an amazing bonding and learning experience and better prepared each individual team member for their future.

2013

2013's BEST competition, GateKeeper, took the team into the field of computer technology. They competed in a timed environment to build a CPU (Central Processing Unit) by remotely controlling a robot to manipulate and assemble electronic parts. The team marketed themselves and their product with a marketing presentation, exhibit, and technical notebook.

2012

The team was tasked with building a multi-functional vehicle for the Space Elevator. The vehicle had to grab various equipment from the base station, carry it up the cable to the midway station, and install it there, all within a stringent time limit. As in every BEST challenge, the competition included marketing before a panel of judges, an exhibit, and a technical notebook.
