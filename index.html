Who are we?

Team

Genesis of Robotics is a home-school robotics team of 15 participating in the 2013 Texas BEST Robotics competition, GateKeeper. We are a team of engineers, artists, and designers.

The Robot

We believe that Genesis of Robotics has constructed a robot of superior quality, one that has scored high on the testing field and at the competition. Engineers have worked to perfect its design for the highest possible score and best quality. Our goal is to develop this machine into a winning prototype that BEST Inc. will choose for its ease of use and ability. Come on competition day to see how our robot has improved!

The Base

The robot's core structure is a tower extending to near the maximum height possible. This tower's rigidity makes the entire machine stable and durable. The base is able to rotate 360 degrees to access materials at any angle.

The Arm

The robot's arm extends and adapts to the challenge, capable of positioning the grasper as far as needed. The arm operates with precision, raising by a powerful winch and extending with exactness. The front part of the arm folds into the 2 foot cube at the beginning of a testing match, then swings out into a locked position for greater reach. This innovative locking mechanism lets the robot fit BEST Inc. specifications while extending our reach to the furthest components.

The Grasper

The robot's grasper is designed to grip transistor containers and logic gates with accuracy and security. The wrist is able to twist 360 degrees to flip transistor containers and position logic gates. The flat edges at the front of the grasper grip integrated circuits, allowing final assembly into CPUs. Our special attachments are able to install two logic gates at once, halfing the assembly time of integrated circuits.

What is BEST?

BEST (Boosting Engineering Science and Technology) gives middle school and high school students nationwide the valuable opportunity to learn about real-world engineering through the design, composition, and marketing of a robot.

The mission of BEST Inc. is to "inspire students to pursue careers in engineering, science, technology, and mathematics through robotics design."
The significance of BEST lies not only in the great opportunity for the students to grow their leadership, teamwork, problem solving, and abstract thinking skills, but also as a way for students to explore various career paths as they prepare for their future in the real world.

Learn more at BESTinc.org

What is our challenge?

2013's BEST competition, GateKeeper, is based on the rapidly growing area of computer technology. Teams are given six weeks to design and build a robot, create a technical notebook, and prepare a tradeshow exhibit and marketing presentation. Then, they will compete in a timed environment to build a CPU (Central Processing Unit) by remotely controlling a robot to manipulate and assemble electronic parts.

The Field

The robot will be mounted on a platform that slides along a track and swings across the factory floor. The operator controls the robot from the side, while the spotter operates the moving platform. The CPU is assembled through multiple rounds of production and in three stages, with components persistant between them.

Stage 1

The robot collects transistor containers and installs them on the assembly line. Depending on how they are installed, the machine will produce a different amount of the respective logic gate.

Stage 2

The robot places logic gates collected in the first stage on the appropriate IC assembly lines. When the right combination of gates are supplied, an integrated circuit is produced.

Stage 3

The robot places the ICs produced in stage 2 on the assembly table to produce a CPU (Central Processing Unit).

History

The Genesis of Robotics Team was organized in 2012 for the BEST Warp XX Challenge. The team formed because of a shared interest in robotics and competition. The BEST competition gave Genesis of Robotics an amazing bonding and learning experience and better prepared each individual team member for their future.

2013

The team is proud of their success at Cowtown BEST, and is preparing themselves for regionals. They intend to improve their performance significantly for success at Texas BEST!

Achievements

  • 1st Place BEST Award
  • 3rd Place Robot Score
  • Advancement to Regionals

2012

The team was tasked with building a multi-functional vehicle for the Space Elevator. The vehicle had to grab various equipment from the base station, carry it up the cable to the midway station, and install it there, all within a stringent time limit. As in every BEST challenge, the competition included marketing before a panel of judges, an exhibit, and a technical notebook.
